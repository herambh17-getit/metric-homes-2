Maharashtra roots

Why Tarkarli & Sindhudurg are the Konkan's smartest coastal buy in 2026

By Metric HomesUpdated June 20267 min read

For years, the Konkan coast was where Mumbai went on holiday — and then drove home. In 2026 that equation has flipped. With an operational airport, a widened highway and year-round tourism demand, Sindhudurg has moved from a season destination to a genuine property market. Here is the case for buying now, in plain terms.

1. The airport changed the maths

The single biggest shift is access. With Sindhudurg (Chipi) airport operational, a region that was an 8–10 hour drive from Mumbai is now a short flight. Airports don't just move tourists — they move the kind of buyer who wants a coastal home but won't tolerate a full-day journey to use it. That demand is what turns a holiday spot into a housing market.

2. NH-66 and the road story

The widening of the Mumbai–Goa highway (NH-66) shortens the self-drive journey and, just as importantly, makes weekend rental trips practical. Better roads raise both the number of visitors and the average length of stay — the two numbers that drive rental yield on a coastal home.

3. Tourism that pays the EMI

Tarkarli and neighbouring Malvan are among the cleanest, most sought-after beaches on the west coast, known for scuba diving, snorkelling and the Sindhudurg fort. That tourism is not seasonal noise — it's a rental engine:

  • Homestays and serviced apartments book out through the long October–May season.
  • Short-stay platforms have measurable, growing demand in the belt.
  • A sea-facing home can work as a personal retreat and a yielding asset between visits.

4. Appreciation: early-cycle, not late

Land and home prices in Sindhudurg are still relatively accessible compared with established coastal markets. Industry commentary places appreciation in the region in the mid-teens to ~20% range year-on-year as infrastructure matures — the pattern of a market in its early cycle rather than a peaked one. Entering before the curve steepens is the whole point.

Appreciation figures are commentary and estimates, not guarantees. Coastal property returns depend on location, title clarity, build quality and timing — do your own diligence, and buy a RERA-registered project.

5. Why "sea-facing" is not a cliché here

On the Konkan coast, the view is the asset. A genuinely sea-facing, well-built home commands a premium on both rent and resale that an inland unit simply cannot. But "sea-facing" only holds its value if the building is engineered to survive the salt and the monsoon — which is a construction question, not a marketing one.
