The project

A home by the sea,
built to last by the sea.

Most coastal apartments are built for a brochure photograph, then surrender to the salt within a decade. Horizon Grande is built the other way round.

Two G+7 towers set back from the Tarkarli shoreline, with every living room oriented to frame the Arabian Sea. The structure is RCC M30 grade with salt-resistant treatment; windows are marine-grade uPVC, double-sealed against monsoon driving rain; steel is corrosion-protected throughout. These aren't upgrades — they're the specification, named in your sale agreement.

Inside, layouts are solved by a qualified architect: cross-ventilated, glare-managed, with storage designed in rather than bolted on. 82 homes in total, sized 760 to 1,180 sq.ft. carpet — proportioned so a family of four lives well in August and December alike.

And because this is Metric, the proof comes before the promise: walk the live site, meet the named site engineer, leave with the all-in cost sheet and the RERA documents in hand.
